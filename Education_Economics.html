1. Which of the following is not a characteristic of a perfectly competitive market?

B) Homogeneous (identical) products
C) Barriers to entry and exit
D) Perfect information

A) GNP (Gross National Product)
B) NDP (Net Domestic Product)
C) GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
D) NNP (Net National Product)
3. Which of the following is considered a factor of production in economics?
A) Money
B) Technology
C) Consumption
D) Advertising
4. What is the economic term for the total value of all goods and services produced by a country's economy within a given period, after deducting the depreciation of capital?
A) Gross National Product (GNP)
B) Net National Product (NNP)
C) Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
D) Net Domestic Product (NDP)

C) Public debt

B) Decrease in the general price level of goods and services
C) Increase in the purchasing power of money
D) Increase in the unemployment rate

A) Fiscal policy
B) Monetary policy
C) Supply-side policy
D) Exchange rate policy

A) Monopoly
B) Oligopoly
C) Perfect competition
D) Monopolistic competition

A) Equilibrium
B) Surplus
C) Shortage
D) Price ceiling
